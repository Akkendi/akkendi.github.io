Masks mandatory: Miami-Dade beaches to reopen amid COVID-19, but with new rules

BY MARTIN VASSOLO and AARON LEIBOWITZ

Face masks will be required to enter Miami-Dade County beaches when they reopen June 1 but not when visitors are in the water or maintaining social distance, according to a draft guide to reopen beaches circulated by the county. Sunbathing, walking and jogging will be allowed, as will the renting of beach concessions like lounge chairs and umbrellas. But no groups bigger than 10 people will be allowed to gather, and common areas like picnic tables and playgrounds will stay off limits. Signs will be posted at the beach highlighting “do and don’t” activities and warning of the “consequences for non-adherence,” like removal from the beach.
